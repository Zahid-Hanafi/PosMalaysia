Section 12.0

SWOT Analysis of the SAP ERP System

A comprehensive strategic evaluation of the system's internal capabilities and external vulnerabilities.

💪 Strengths

  • Centralised Architecture: Seamlessly joins massive HR and Finance modules into a single, unified corporate platform.
  • High Capacity: Confidently handles massive workloads, including 15,000 monthly payrolls and 100,000 billing invoices.
  • Proven Stability: Has successfully managed 11 terabytes of operational data since its implementation in 2013.
  • Reporting Power: Provides executives with real-time monitoring and highly structured strategic reporting capabilities.

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Complex Interface: Notoriously steep learning curve that often frustrates new employees until they adapt.
  • Specialized Skills: Requires users to hold highly specialized, formal SAP certifications to operate efficiently.
  • High Costs: Licensing and regular system maintenance consume approximately RM1.6 million annually.
  • Understaffed Support: Maintained by a surprisingly small internal IT team of only 8 staff for such a critical system.

🚀 Opportunities

  • S/4HANA Migration: Pos Malaysia could migrate to SAP's latest S/4HANA version for drastically improved in-memory processing speeds.
  • AI Integration: Potential to integrate Artificial Intelligence to completely automate repetitive tasks like invoice matching.
  • Advanced Analytics: Deeper connection with Big Data tools could provide predictive workforce and budget planning.

🔥 Threats

  • System Overload: As e-commerce and parcel volumes surge post-pandemic, the hardware risks severe overloading.
  • Cyberattacks: Holding 11TB of sensitive financial and personal employee data makes the system a highly lucrative target for hackers.
  • Vendor Dependency: Extreme reliance on external SAP vendors means Pos Malaysia is highly vulnerable to sudden licensing changes.
  • Data Loss Risk: Any unplanned downtime or server crash could instantly disrupt the nation's logistics network.
Section 13.0

Generate and Evaluate Alternatives

Proposing strategic solutions to mitigate the system's critical weaknesses and external threats.

13.1 Alternative 1: Backup & Disaster Recovery

  • →Tiered Strategy: Implementation of full, incremental, and differential backups.
  • →Hybrid Storage: Storing backups safely across on-premises servers and the AWS cloud.
  • →Data Security: Mandatory, military-grade encryption of all backend backup data.
  • →Actionable Drills: Conducting mandatory disaster recovery testing drills every 6 months to ensure readiness.

13.2 Alternative 2: Cybersecurity & Monitoring

  • →Active Security: Deploying Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and comprehensive SIEM tools.
  • →Strict Auditing: Contracting external firms for regular penetration testing and vulnerability checks.
  • →Live Monitoring: Deploying advanced tracking software for CPU load spikes and slow database query speeds.
  • →Team Expansion: Expanding the current 8-person IT team to ensure rapid, 24/7 incident response.

Section 14.0

Recommendations & Action Plan

14.1 Recommended Solution

Final Recommendation: Implement BOTH alternatives simultaneously.

  • Perfect Synergy: The alternatives are highly complementary. Alternative 2 prevents incidents from occurring, while Alternative 1 guarantees you can recover if they do.
  • Complete Protection: Combined, they directly address the most critical vulnerabilities of the 11TB database—securing it against both physical hardware failure and digital hacking.
  • Operational Tweaks: We also recommend enhancing specialized module training for new hires to reduce the initial learning curve, and increasing reporting automation.

14.2 Six-Month Action Plan Timeline

Phase 1 (Months 1-3)

Disaster Recovery & Backup

Establish strict Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs). Deploy tiered hybrid backups across the AWS cloud. Finalize the formally documented Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP).

Phase 2 (Months 1-3, Concurrent)

Cybersecurity & Active Monitoring

Conduct a full security audit of SAP interfaces. Deploy SIEM tools and tighten role-based access controls. Configure automated SMS alerts for severe performance spikes.

Phase 3 (Months 4-6)

Testing, Validation & Handoff

Execute the first formal disaster recovery failover drill. Schedule the first quarterly penetration test. Finalize the hiring process to expand the IT capacity team.
