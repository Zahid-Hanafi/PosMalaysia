Section 9.0

Hardware and Software Configuration

An overview of the massive technical infrastructure required to support Pos Malaysia's daily national operations.

🖥️ 9.1 Hardware Configuration

  • Hybrid Infrastructure: Initially fully on-premise, now utilizing a mix of physical data centre servers and AWS cloud hosting.
  • Robust Network: Relies on high-speed Local Area Networks (LAN) internally and a secure Wide Area Network (WAN) to link nationwide branches.
  • System Reliability: Heavily reliant on High Availability (HA) configurations and redundant backup servers to prevent catastrophic data loss during outages.

💿 9.2 Software Configuration

  • Core ERP System: The SAP ERP platform (introduced in 2013) serves as the primary software backbone connecting all HR and Finance activities.
  • Massive Database: Employs an RDBMS SQL database to structure and store 11 terabytes of historical and operational data.
  • Deep Integration: Seamlessly communicates with 165 other internal systems (like logistics and CRM) instantly via APIs.

Section 10.0

Information System Problems

A massive system inevitably comes with massive challenges that require constant management.

10.1 Current Issues and Challenges

▼

🔴 Overwhelming Complexity

The SAP system is not user-friendly for beginners. It requires employees to undergo intensive training and hold professional SAP certification just to perform their daily tasks efficiently.

🟠 Slow Peak Performance

Due to the sheer volume of data, the system experiences noticeable "overload" delays during peak workload periods, specifically during month-end payroll or mass billing cycles.

🟡 Extremely High Costs

Licensing and routine maintenance cost approximately RM1.6 million annually. Furthermore, applying system updates is a delicate, highly expensive process requiring external vendors.

🟣 Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities

As the central repository for the nation's sensitive financial and employee data, the SAP database is a constant, high-value target for external cyber threats and ransomware.

10.2 Root Causes of the Problems

▼
  • System Architecture Flaws: The deeply interconnected modules mean that a technical error in HR can cause a "domino effect" that crashes the Finance module as well.
  • Massive Transaction Workload: Attempting to process up to 100,000 invoices concurrently pushes the physical server hardware beyond its optimal capacity, causing slowdowns.
  • Specialized Skills Gap: Employees are required to possess a very rare combination of both IT proficiency and deep accounting knowledge to use the system without making errors.

10.3 Direct Impact on Performance

▼
  • Reduced Efficiency: Workflow bottlenecks during peak processing times force employees to wait for the system to catch up, delaying their daily tasks.
  • Service Quality Drops: Slow system responses across the integrated API network mean that real-time customer tracking updates may be delayed.
  • Hampered Decision-Making: When system errors occur or the database slows down, managers face critical delays in receiving accurate, real-time reporting data.

11.1 High System Efficiency

  • Fully automates the complex payroll for 15,000 staff, completing a job in minutes that would otherwise take weeks manually.
  • Handles massive, continuous data scaling without requiring Pos Malaysia to hire additional clerical manpower.
  • Drastically reduces data duplication because information entered by HR is instantly available to Finance.

11.2 High System Effectiveness

  • Creates a seamless, undeniable link between Human Resources operations and corporate Financial ledgers.
  • Provides executives with highly accurate, real-time strategic reports to base million-dollar decisions upon.
  • Effectively minimises human accounting errors by relying on strict, automated pricing and calculation rules.

11.3 User Satisfaction Levels

  • Once fully trained, staff report very high satisfaction due to the convenience of having everything in a centralized, single-platform design.
  • Users highly appreciate the speed of automated tasks and the complete elimination of tedious manual paperwork.
  • Drawback: The initial learning curve is notoriously steep, frustrating new hires until they receive formal certification.

11.4 Operational Contribution

  • It has served reliably as the firm's primary digital backbone since 2013, supporting a decade of corporate growth.
  • Greatly enhances daily operational efficiency by allowing departments to collaborate across a unified dataset.
  • Ensures that ground logistics and customer service teams have the financial backing necessary for reliable service.
