Section 2.0

Organization Background

A deep dive into the history, structure, and massive scale of Malaysia's national postal and logistics provider.

🏢 Company Overview

  • National Provider: The sole national postal and premier logistics service provider in Malaysia.
  • Beyond Letters: Evolved from standard mail delivery to comprehensive courier services, warehousing, e-commerce support, and digital solutions.
  • Massive Network: Operates through a widespread network of more than 3,500 physical touchpoints and agencies across the country.

🌟 Vision & Mission

Vision: "We Deliver. We Connect. We Improve Lives."
Demonstrating ambition to be more than a simple postal provider by making customers' lives richer.

Mission: "Passionate about building trust to connect lives and businesses for a better tomorrow."
Focusing heavily on the positive socioeconomic impact Pos Malaysia brings to local communities.

2.2 History

Early 1800s & 1957

Postal Beginnings

Initiated in the British Straits Settlements (Penang, Malacca, Singapore) and continued as Jabatan Perkhidmatan Pos after Malaysia's 1957 independence.

1992 & 2001

Corporatisation & Listing

Shifted from a government agency to a commercially oriented company in 1992. Successfully listed publicly on the KLSE in August 2001.

2021 & 2022

Modern Transformation

Launched the "Transform the Core" initiative in 2021, leading to a massive digital migration of critical infrastructure to the AWS cloud in 2022.

2.3 Revenue Structure

Total RM1.87 billion (FY2023)

Section 5.0

System Classification

Classifying the 6 key systems based on their specific functional level within the organization.

5.1 SAP ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)

▼
  • Class: Both an Operational IS and a Management IS.
  • Role: Manages heavy daily HR calculation tasks while simultaneously providing highly structured, high-level reports for management oversight.

5.2 Billing System

▼
  • Class: Transaction Processing System (TPS).
  • Role: Designed to automate and record extremely high volumes of routine, daily transactions specifically for mass corporate invoicing.

5.3 Courier Management System

▼
  • Class: Operational Information System (OIS).
  • Role: Focused intensely on day-to-day ground operations, specifically tracking the physical movement of mail and parcels from A to B.

5.4 Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

▼
  • Class: Management Information System (MIS).
  • Role: Manages customer data, interaction records, and service histories to help improve customer satisfaction.

5.5 Big Data Analytics System

▼
  • Class: Decision Support System (DSS).
  • Role: Analyzes millions of records to provide structured insights that guide executives in long-term strategic planning.

5.6 Point of Sale (POS) System

▼
  • Class: Transaction Processing System (TPS).
  • Role: Processes thousands of walk-in counter transactions at post office branches daily (e.g., bill payments, stamps).
